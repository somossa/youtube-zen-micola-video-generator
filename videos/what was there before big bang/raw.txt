Imagine you have a time machine.
Not a normal one.
A perfect time machine.
One that can travel back through every moment in history.
You go back one year.
Then one thousand years.
Then a million.
Dinosaurs appear.
Then the Earth disappears completely.
You keep going.
The Sun hasn't formed yet.
The Milky Way doesn't exist.
No stars.
No planets.
No galaxies.
Just a hot, dense universe shrinking smaller and smaller as you travel backward through time.
You keep going.
Ten billion years.
Twelve billion years.
Thirteen billion years.
And then...
You reach the Big Bang.
The moment everything began.
The moment space itself was born.
The moment time itself started ticking.
Naturally, the next question is:
What was there before that?
And this is where things get weird.
Because according to our current understanding of physics...
The answer might actually be...
Nothing.

Not empty space.

Not darkness.

Not a giant explosion waiting to happen.

Nothing.

Because the Big Bang wasn't an explosion that happened inside space.

The Big Bang was the creation of space itself.

Think about it this way.

Asking what was before the Big Bang might be like asking:

"What's north of the North Pole?"

At first, it sounds like a reasonable question.

But the closer you look, the less sense it makes.

The North Pole is the northernmost point.

There is no north beyond it.

Similarly, if time itself began at the Big Bang, there may have been no "before" for anything to exist in.

No time.

No events.

No waiting.

No before.

But not every scientist agrees that's the end of the story.

Some theories suggest there actually was something before the Big Bang.

One idea is called the Big Bounce.

According to this theory, our universe may not be the first universe.

Instead, a previous universe existed long before ours.

Over trillions of years, it slowly collapsed inward.

Smaller.

Denser.

Hotter.

Until eventually it reached an extreme state and bounced outward again.

Creating the universe we see today.

In this picture, the Big Bang wasn't the beginning.

It was just a restart button.

Like the universe taking a cosmic breath.

In.

Out.

Again and again forever.

But wait.

It gets even stranger.

Another theory suggests our universe might be only one bubble in an enormous cosmic ocean.

Imagine boiling water.

Bubbles constantly appear and disappear.

Now replace the water with an unimaginably vast reality.

And replace the bubbles with entire universes.

Each one having its own stars.

Its own galaxies.

Maybe even its own laws of physics.

Our universe could simply be one bubble among countless others.

And if that's true...

The Big Bang may have been the moment our particular bubble was born.

Not the moment everything began.

Then there's perhaps the strangest possibility of all.

Some physicists think time itself may be an illusion.

At the deepest level of reality, there may be no past.

No future.

No flow of time at all.

What we experience as time could simply emerge from something more fundamental that we don't yet understand.

In that case, asking what happened before the Big Bang could be like asking what color the number seven is.

The question itself may not fit the rules of reality.

And here's the truth.

Nobody knows which answer is correct.

Not yet.

The Big Bang is where our current theories begin to break down.

General relativity tells one story.

Quantum mechanics tells another.

And when we push both theories back to the very beginning...

The math stops making sense.

Somewhere beyond that wall lies one of the greatest mysteries in science.

So what was there before the Big Bang?

Maybe nothing.

Maybe another universe.

Maybe an infinite cycle of cosmic rebirths.

Maybe countless universes beyond our own.

Or maybe the question itself has no answer because time didn't exist yet.

The most incredible part is that right now, somewhere on Earth, scientists are searching for clues.

Studying ancient light.

Building powerful telescopes.

Trying to uncover evidence that could reveal what happened before the beginning.

And perhaps one day, humanity will finally know.

Until then...

The biggest mystery in the universe remains the one that came before the universe itself.
